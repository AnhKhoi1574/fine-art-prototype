BUILDING 1

The Museum opened in May 1989 following facilities and collection organization. The Museum has 21,000 treasures. The museum's historic, traditional, and contemporary art includes resistance drawings, Indochina, Gia Dinh, Nguyen Gia Tri, Nguyen Sang, Diep Minh Chau, Kim Bach, Dinh Ru, and Quach Phong. Ho Chi Minh and south Vietnam. Event is vital to exhibiting city's great art. Ho Chi Minh City and its environs attract 200,000 visitors yearly.

BUILDING 2

The Museum hosts local and international exhibits in building 2. On average, there are 10-25 shows each year, comprising ASEAN works and paintings by Russian, Chinese, American, Italian, Canadian, Japanese, Thai, Singaporean, Korean, and Indonesian artists.

BUILDING 3

Building 3 showcases pottery, metal, wood, and stone objects from ancient and current times. Southern pottery lines including Lai Thieu, Saigon, Bien Hoa, and Dong Nai river pottery are on show at the Museum. Stone art: The museum has Cham Pa and Oc Eo statues. The Museum of bronze art. Display and collect religious and domestic wooden object.
