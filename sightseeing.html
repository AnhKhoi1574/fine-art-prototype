INTRODUCE

Fine Arts Museum of City. The People's Committee of Ho Chi Minh City formed the city on September 5, 1987, with Decision No. 194/QD-UB. Located in 97A Pho Duc Chinh, Nguyen Thai Binh Ward, District I, Ho Chi Minh City. After preparing facilities and items, the Museum opened to visitors in May 1989. The Museum has around 22,000 antiquities in numerous collections. Ancient and modern art galleries feature works by Indochina and Gia Dinh school painters, Nguyen Gia Tri and Nguyen Sang, Diep Minh Chau, Kim Bach, Dinh Ru, Quach Phong, and others, reflecting city art. Southern Vietnam and Ho Chi Minh.

RULES FOR VISITORS

  • Dress politely when visiting the Museum.
  • Visitors to visit, and contact businesses must park in the right place.
  • Visitors who need voice acting, please contact the voiceover or at the ticket counter.
  • Tour guides must wear tags when guiding visitors at the Museum.
  • Do not bring dangerous weapons.
  • Do not smoke, and do not make noise while visiting.
  • Do not touch or touch the artifacts.
  • Food and drinks are not allowed.
  • Cell phones are only allowed to take pictures.

MAP & ENTRANCE

The late 19th and early 20th century French architecture of the Museum of Fine Arts seamlessly combined Eastern architecture in external décor, roof tiles, ceramic columns, and walls. The roof has ceramic embellishments. These houses have numerous creative and attractive things, including conch wooden furniture, skillfully carved woodwork, and pottery.
